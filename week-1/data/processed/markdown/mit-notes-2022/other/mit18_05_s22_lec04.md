Meaning of Expected Value
Example. Suppose an experiment produces random values 80, 1, 10
with the following table
value: 80 1 10
pmf: 1/5 1/5 3/5
What is the expected average value over many experiments?
Solution: Say 5 experiments: 80,80,10,10,10. Ave. = 190/5 = 38
In 5 experiments, average will vary a lot. What about in 500?
Expect each number occurs roughly in proportion to its probability.
Suppose this is exactly what happens and compute the average.
value: 80 1 10
expected counts: 100 100 300
100⋅80+100 ⋅1+300 ⋅10 1 1 3
average = = ⋅80+ ⋅1+ ⋅10 = 22.2
500 5 5 5
This is the ‘expected average’ = expected value or expectation.

Deluge of discrete distributions: Geometric(𝑝)
𝑋 ∼ Geometric(𝑝) = # of failures before first success in a
sequence of independent Bernoulli(𝑝) trials
Values: 0, 1, 2, 3, …
PMF: 𝑝(𝑘) = 𝑝(1 − 𝑝)𝑘

Neutral language helps avoid confusion over whether we want the
number of successes before the first failure or vice versa.

P(red) remains the same.
