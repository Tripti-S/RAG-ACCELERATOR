When given a 95% confidence interval many people often mistake this to mean that there is
a 95% probablility that the true value of the parameter is in the confidence interval. This
violates the maxim that frequentists don’t give probabilities for hypotheses, but the reasoning
is subtle. This problem is aimed at understanding the subtlety.
(a) What 95% does mean. Imagine running the following experiment.

(iii) The confidence interval provides an estimate of the true value of the average increase
in survival time.
(iv) The null hypothesis that there is no change in survival time due to the new treatment
