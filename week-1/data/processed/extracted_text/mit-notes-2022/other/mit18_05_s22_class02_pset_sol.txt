Problem 2. (Inclusion-exclusion)
Supposes a class has 50 students: 20 male (M), 25 brown-eyed (B)
For a randomly chosen student, what is the range of possible values for рқ‘қ = рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ вҲӘрқҗө)?
(a) рқ‘қ вүӨ 0.4
(b) 0.4 вүӨ рқ‘қ вүӨ 0.5
(c) 0.4 вүӨ рқ‘қ вүӨ 0.9
(d) 0.5 вүӨ рқ‘қ вүӨ 0.9
(e) 0.5 вүӨ рқ‘қ
Solution: (d) 0.5 вүӨ рқ‘қ вүӨ 0.9
Explanation:
The easy way to answer this is that рқҗҙ вҲӘ рқҗө has a minumum of 25 members (when all males
are brown-eyed) and a maximum of 45 members (when no males have brown-eyes). So, the
probability ranges from 0.5 to 0.9
Thinking about it in terms of the inclusion-exclusion principle we have
рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ вҲӘрқҗө) = рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ)+рқ‘ғ(рқҗө)вҲ’рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ вҲ©рқҗө) = 0.9вҲ’рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ вҲ©рқҗө).
So the maximum possible value of рқ‘ғ (рқ‘Җ вҲӘрқҗө) happens if M and B are disjoint, so рқ‘ғ (рқ‘Җ вҲ©рқҗө) =
0. The minimum happens when рқ‘Җ вҠӮ рқҗө, so рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ вҲ©рқҗө) = рқ‘ғ(рқ‘Җ) = 0.4.
